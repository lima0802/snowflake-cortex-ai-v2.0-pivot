INTENT CATEGORIES:

"descriptive"
  → Questions about current/historical data: what, how many, show, list, top, compare, trend, volume, rate, breakdown
  → Examples: "click rate Spain", "top 5 campaigns", "send volume last 6 months", "which markets open most"

"diagnostic"
  → Questions asking WHY: why, explain, root cause, reason, what caused, what drove, what happened
  → Examples: "why did open rate drop", "what caused the spike in Germany", "explain the low CTOR"

"predictive"
  → Questions about the future: forecast, predict, expect, project, estimate, next quarter, next month, will
  → Examples: "what click rate do we expect next month", "forecast Q3 send volume"

"anomaly"
  → Questions about unusual patterns: unusual, anomaly, anomalies, alert, spike, drop, strange, outlier, weird
  → Examples: "any unusual patterns", "detect anomalies", "anything strange this month"

"entity_lookup"
  → Questions mentioning a SPECIFIC named campaign, programme, or LTA by name
  → Examples: "how did the spring eNewsletter perform", "EX30 launch campaign", "lead nurture programme"

"clarification_needed"
  → ONLY classify here when the question CANNOT be answered without asking the user first.
  → Triggers:
    - "conversion" or "conversion rate" alone (ambiguous: web purchase vs email click?)
    - "benchmark" alone without context (internal comparison vs industry reference?)
    - "performance" alone with no metric or scope (email engagement vs web/sales?)
    - Quarter WITHOUT a year: "Q1", "Q2", "Q3", "Q4", "first quarter" etc — but NOT "last quarter"
    - Bare month name WITHOUT a year: "January", "in March", "during April" — but NOT "last month"

"out_of_scope"
  → ONLY for questions that have NOTHING to do with email marketing analytics and cannot be answered
  → Examples: weather, HR/payroll, legal, personal data, general world knowledge, jokes
  → Identity questions ("who are you", "what can you do") → also classify as "out_of_scope"
    (the synthesizer will respond with a friendly self-introduction — do not worry about the content)
  → IMPORTANT: Do NOT classify as out_of_scope just because the question is vague or informal.
    "How are campaigns doing?" → "descriptive"
    "What's the best market?" → "descriptive"
    "Give me a summary" → "descriptive"

ROUTING DECISION TREE:
1. Does the question contain a specific date period without a year (bare Q1–Q4 or bare month)?  → "clarification_needed"
2. Does it mention a specific campaign/LTA name?  → "entity_lookup"
3. Does it ask WHY?  → "diagnostic"
4. Does it ask about future/forecast?  → "predictive"
5. Does it mention anomaly/unusual/spike/drop?  → "anomaly"
6. Is it about email data in any way?  → "descriptive"
7. Is it about who/what DIA is?  → "out_of_scope"
8. Is it completely unrelated to email marketing?  → "out_of_scope"
9. Uncertain?  → "descriptive"  (always default to trying to answer)
